Have you changed the default usernames and passwords on your smart devices? (e.g., Smart TV, Alexa/Siri, Smart lights, Doorbells etc)
Why this matters
Smart devices often come with default usernames and passwords that are the same for every device. Hackers know these and can easily break in if you don‚Äôt change them. Changing the login details helps protect your devices from being accessed by strangers.
How to check

1. Open the app or webpage that controls your smart device.
2. Look in the settings or account/security section for login details.
3. If you see ‚Äúadmin‚Äù or similar as the username, and no password or a simple one like ‚Äú1234‚Äù ‚Äì it‚Äôs likely still the default.

Do your smart devices automatically install updates to stay secure?
Why this matters
Updates (more specifically, Firmware Updates) fix bugs and close security holes that hackers could exploit. Without them, your smart device may stay vulnerable to known attacks.

1. Open the app that controls your smart device.
2. Look under Settings or Device Info for options like "Firmware", "Software Update", or "Automatic Updates".
3. Turn on auto-update if it‚Äôs available.

üîë Password Hygiene

Do you use different passwords for important accounts (email, banking, shopping)?
‚ñº
Do you use different passwords for important accounts?
Why this matters
If one site is breached, re-used passwords can let attackers into your other accounts.
How to check

1) List your key accounts; note if any share the same password.
2) If yes, change them to unique passwords (use a manager for convenience).

Do you use a password manager to store and generate passwords?
‚ñº
Do you use a password manager to store and generate passwords?
Why this matters
Managers reduce password re-use and help you use stronger, unique passwords without needing to remember them all.
How to check

1) If you store passwords in notes or reuse them, install a manager (e.g., built-in browser store or dedicated app).
2) Move key accounts into the manager and enable autofill/alerts.

Do you use two-factor authentication (2FA) on your important accounts (email, banking, socials)??
‚ñº
Do you use two-factor authentication (2FA) on your important accounts (email, banking, socials)??
Why this matters
2FA adds an extra layer of security, making it harder for attackers to access your accounts even if they get your password.
How to check

1) Open account Security settings (email/bank/social).
2) Enable Two-Factor Authentication (prefer an authenticator app over SMS if available).
3) Store backup codes safely.

Do you check if your email(s) appear in known data breaches and change passwords if found?
‚ñº
Do you check if your email(s) appear in known data breaches and change passwords if found?
Why this matters
If your email appears in a breach, attackers may try your passwords elsewhere (‚Äúcredential stuffing‚Äù).
How to check

1) Visit haveibeenpwned.com and enter your email.
2) If breached, change those passwords and enable 2FA.
3) Turn on breach alerts (via manager or service).

Have you changed the default router admin password and your Wi-Fi network name (SSID)?
Why this matters
Default router passwords are widely known and often targeted by attackers to take over your network.
How to check

1) Open your router app or browse to its address (e.g., 192.168.0.1) which is usually on the back of your router.
2) Login and change admin password; then rename SSID and set a strong Wi-Fi password (WPA2/WPA3).

Is your router firmware up to date (automatic updates enabled where available)?
Why this matters
Updates fix (patch) known vulnerabilities to prevent attackers actively exploiting them .
How to check

1) Router app/web ‚Üí Administration/Firmware.
2) Turn on auto-updates or click Check for updates and apply.

Do you regularly review connected devices and remove ones you don‚Äôt recognise?

2) Set WPS to Off (or disable PIN).
